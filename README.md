To interpret user needs and implement them into a program, first I need user stories to tell me in their words what they are experiencing and what they would like to see. 
Once I have the user stories, I can then create sprint goals and prioritize them, and estimate how much work and time will be going into each. User stories provide development teams 
with insight from users that is crucial to creating a good quality and relevant product.

I approach development programs in a way that aligns to the Agile practices because I find it easiest to tackle big goals when it is broken down into smaller tasks. I also value the
importance of team communication and collaboration, so in my development projects I would ensure there is a solid communication platform everyone can be heard on, and we discuss goals
and priorities via daily check ins and startup meetings. I hope to bring these habits to my development team because they lead a team to success and provide more oppoortunities for 
better quality products that other methods fall short on.

Being a good team member in a software development team means knowing your role and executing it in an appropriate manner. Each team member has different roles and responsibilities in
the team, so it is important to know your role very well so other people can rely on you to contribute your portion. The other key to being a good team member is communicating as much
as you can to your team members so everyone is always on the same page. If an issue pops up, or you just have a general comment or question about the product, you should always speak 
up.
